SECTION 01

Introduction

Background Problem

The Problem: Too Many Cables

Imagine having a desk cluttered with charging cables for your phone, laptop, smartwatch, and wireless earbuds. Each device needs its own cable, and you're constantly hunting for the right charger.

Key Challenge: Traditional charging methods require physical connections, creating friction in daily use.

This creates several problems:

  • Cable wear and tear
  • Incompatible standards
  • Limited mobility while charging
  • Safety hazards from damaged cables

What if you could walk into a room and have all your devices charge automatically?

→ User pain point clearly defined

SECTION 02

The Invention

Core Tech Solution

Wireless Power Through Resonance

This invention uses electromagnetic resonance to charge multiple devices simultaneously without physical connection.

Think of it like a Wi-Fi router for power. Just as your phone connects to Wi-Fi automatically when in range, devices charge automatically near the power transmitter.

Two Components:

Transmitter: Creates oscillating magnetic field

Receiver: Captures energy from field

→ "Wi-Fi for power" analogy works well

SECTION 03

How It Works

Technical Mechanism

Step-by-Step Process

1. Transmission: Base creates oscillating magnetic field at 6.78 MHz

2. Resonance: Device with tuned receiver enters field, resonance occurs (like tuning forks)

3. Conversion: Receiver converts field to electrical current, regulated for battery charging

4. Optimization: Sensors monitor efficiency, adjust power to minimize waste

→ Tuning fork analogy = key insight
