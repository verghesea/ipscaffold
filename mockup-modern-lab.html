Section 02

The Invention

Wireless Power Through Resonance

This invention introduces a wireless power transfer system that uses electromagnetic resonance to charge multiple devices simultaneously without any physical connection.

Think of it like a Wi-Fi router for power instead of internet. Just as your phone connects to Wi-Fi automatically when you're in range, this system allows devices to charge automatically when they're within a certain distance of the power transmitter.

The system consists of two main components:

  • Transmitter Unit: A base station that creates an oscillating magnetic field
  • Receiver Coils: Small components embedded in your devices that capture energy from this field

Section 03

Detailed Functioning

How It Actually Works

The technology operates on the principle of magnetic resonance coupling. Here's the step-by-step process:

1. Power Transmission
The base station generates an alternating current through its coil, creating an oscillating magnetic field at a specific frequency (typically 6.78 MHz, an industry standard).

2. Resonance Coupling
When a device with a receiver coil tuned to the same frequency enters this magnetic field, resonance occursâ€”like how a tuning fork vibrates when another matching tuning fork is struck nearby.

3. Power Conversion
The receiver coil converts the magnetic field back into electrical current, which is then regulated to the appropriate voltage for charging the device's battery.

4. Efficiency Optimization
Smart sensors continuously monitor the coupling efficiency and adjust the transmission power to minimize energy waste and prevent overheating.

Section 04

Importance

Why This Matters

This wireless charging system represents a significant advancement over existing solutions for several reasons:

Greater Range: Unlike current wireless chargers that require devices to be placed on a charging pad, this system works across distances of up to 30 centimeters, allowing more flexibility in device placement.

Multiple Device Support: The system can charge up to 5 devices simultaneously, automatically distributing power based on each device's needs.

Universal Compatibility: By adhering to the Qi wireless charging standard while extending its capabilities, this technology works with existing compatible devices.

Safety Features: Built-in sensors detect foreign objects and living tissue in the charging field, automatically reducing power to prevent interference with medical devices or safety concerns.
